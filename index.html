A Guide to Leather Jackets


Want to be a part of a longstanding tradition within Engineering? Well look no further! Every year, once a semester, the Engineering Stores hosts jacket fittings for all students who have finished their first year!
Within Canada, most engineering schools have their own Engineering jackets; all in various colours and styles. The University of Toronto Engineering Jacket has gone through several renditions over many decades, including a corduroy style! For the foreseeable future, our Engineering jackets are a black leather, available in either a bomber style fit or a tailored fit! You can customize your jacket with unique designs, patches and other options to add your own style!
This guide is meant to be a summary of what you need to know about customizing your own jacket, and to give you an idea for what you want to put on yours!

Customizations



Arm Stitching


The discipline and year stitching included in the base price is for up to 7 letters/numbers.

Additional lines can be added at an additional cost (such as “T1” or “PEY”) of $4 per letter.

The other arm can be whatever you’d like it to be. Common choices include:

  • ERTW (Engineers Rule The World)
  • E4TW (Engineers 4 The World)
  • SPS (School of Practical Science)
  • PEY (Professional Experience Year)
  • A Nickname
  • More Patches

Note: It is recommended not to go longer than 4 letters for either arm unless one letter is small (such as “i”)

Patches


You can purchase your patches in advance or with the jacket. Sewing of one patch is included in the base price.
